1. Reward race overview

Thank you for choosing the Reward Race loyalty program! With our program, you can offer your customers exciting ways to earn points and keep them engaged with your store.

The Reward Race program is a fun and engaging loyalty system designed to help merchants reward customers for their actions. It encourages customers to interact with your business, earn points, and compete for exciting rewards.

1.1. What is the reward race program ?

The Reward Race program is a fun and engaging loyalty system designed to help merchants reward customers for their actions. It encourages customers to interact with your business, earn points, and compete for exciting rewards.

1.2. What makes us unique

Our system offers four key features— Reward Program, VIP Program, Referral Program, and Survey Program— designed to help merchants easily reward their customers and boost engagement. Here's how each feature works and how you can use them to award points:

  • 1. Reward Program: Encourage everyday customer actions like purchases or social media interactions.
  • Example: A customer spends $50 at your store and earns 500 points, which they can later use to get a 10% discount.
  • 1. VIP Program: Reward your most loyal customers with exclusive benefits. Set different VIP tiers (e.g., Silver, Gold, Platinum) based on customer spending or points earned. Offer extra rewards, such as bonus points, early access to sales, or special gifts for higher tiers.
  • Example: A customer who earns 1,000 points in a month qualifies for the Gold tier and receives double points on all purchases for the next month.
  • 1. Referral Program: Grow your customer base through word-of-mouth marketing. Provide customers with a unique referral code or link. When someone uses their code to make a purchase, both the referrer and the new customer earn points.
  • Example: A customer refers a friend who spends $30. The customer earns 300 points, and the friend gets a welcome bonus of 100 points.
  • 1. Survey Program: Collect valuable feedback while rewarding customers for their input. Create surveys about your products or services. Offer points to customers who complete the surveys, encouraging participation.
  • Example: A customer fills out a feedback form about their shopping experience and earns 50 points.

  4. Clearly communicate the rewards program on your loyalty page and at checkout to ensure customers understand the benefits.

2.1.4 Social Share Rewards

"Social Share Rewards" is an excellent feature to drive customer engagement on social media. Customers can earn points for interacting with your brand's social accounts, such as liking, sharing, or following. This not only boosts customer loyalty but also increases your brand’s visibility and reach online. Below are the specific social media actions you can set up with their respective rewards:

1. Facebook Like

Reward customers with points for liking your Facebook page. This is a simple yet effective way to grow your follower base and stay connected with your audience, keeping them updated on your latest products and offers.

Here’s how you can set it up:

  • 1. Set a valid Facebook page URL: Make sure the URL you provide links directly to your business’s official Facebook page.
  • 2. Assign a points value: Decide how many points customers will earn for liking your page. For example, awarding 10–20 points can be a great incentive without being overly generous.

2. Follow on Instagram

Reward customers for following your Instagram account. This not only helps you build a loyal community but also ensures your followers stay updated with your latest products, promotions, and offers.

Here’s how you can set it up:

  • 1. Set a valid Instagram username: Provide the correct username or URL that links directly to your official Instagram account. Double-check to ensure there are no errors.
  • 2. Assign a points value: Choose a reasonable number of points to reward your customers for following your Instagram account. For example, 10–15 points is often an effective incentive.

3. X (Twitter) Follow

Reward customers with points for following your X (formerly Twitter) account. This helps increase your follower base and keeps customers engaged with your brand updates, announcements, and promotions.

Here’s how you can set it up:

  • 1. Provide a valid X (Twitter) username: Enter your official X (Twitter) handle to ensure customers are directed to your business’s account. Double-check for accuracy to avoid broken links or confusion.
  • 2. Assign a points value: Set the number of points customers will earn for following your account. A range of 10–15 points is usually effective in motivating followers.

4. Facebook Share

Reward customers with points for sharing your business on their Facebook account. This helps boost your brand’s visibility, attract new followers, and keep your audience engaged with updates, announcements, and promotions.

Here’s how you can set it up:

  • 1. Set a valid Facebook page URL: Enter the URL of your official Facebook page to ensure customers can easily share your content.
  • 2. Assign a points value: Decide how many points customers will earn for sharing your business. A range of 10–20 points is generally effective in encouraging engagement.

5. X (Twitter) Share

Reward users for sharing your posts or products on X (formerly Twitter). This helps increase your brand’s visibility and can attract new potential leads through your customers' networks.

Here’s how you can set it up:

  • 1. Set a valid X account URL: Provide the URL of your official X account to ensure customers are directed to your business page for sharing.
  • 2. Set a default caption: Add a predefined caption that customers can use when sharing your posts. Ensure the caption is clear, engaging, and highlights your product or offer.
  • 3. Assign a points value: Choose how many points customers will earn for sharing your posts. Typically, 10–20 points work well to encourage participation.

Tips to Maximize Social Share Rewards:

  1. Encourage meaningful actions: Reward customers for actions that add real value, such as sharing product reviews or posts about new launches.
  2. Set achievable rewards: Offer points that are enticing but sustainable, like 50 points for sharing a post or 20 points for following your page.

  4. Monitor performance: Track which social media platforms bring the most engagement and adjust your rewards strategy accordingly.
  5. Leverage trends: Tie social media rewards to trending topics or events to increase participation, e.g., "Earn double points for sharing during Black Friday week!"

2.2.1. Order Discount

Order discounts are a great way to encourage customers to make a purchase by offering them a special discount on their order. To set up an order discount, follow these steps:

1. Create a Discount Code: Start by entering a unique discount code that customers will need to apply during checkout to receive their discount. This code can be shared via email, social media, or other communication channels.

2. Choose the Discount Type:We offer two types of discounts:

  • Fixed Amount Discount: For example, you could offer a discount of $10 off a customer’s order. In this case, the discount amount is fixed, regardless of the total order value.
  • Percentage Discount: For example, if you set a 10% discount, and the customer’s total order is $50, they will receive a $5 discount. The discount is calculated as a percentage of the total order amount.

3. Set Discount Amount: If you select a fixed amount, you’ll need to specify the discount value (e.g., $10). If you choose a percentage, you’ll need to specify the discount percentage (e.g., 10%). Remember that these two options are directly related to the order total or percentage chosen.

4. Minimum Order Value: You’ll also need to set a minimum order value. This means customers must reach a certain order amount before they can use the discount. For example, you can set a minimum order value of $50, which means customers can only use the discount if their total purchase is greater than or equal to $50.

Example: Let’s say you offer a 15% discount on orders over $50. A customer purchases a product worth $100 and applies the discount code. They will receive a $15 discount (15% of $100), making their total $85 (before taxes and shipping).

Here are concise tips for setting up effective order discounts:

Quick Tips for Order Discounts:

  1. Use catchy codes: Create memorable and simple discount codes like "SAVE10" or "WELCOME15" for easy sharing.
  2. Combine discounts strategically: Pair order discounts with free shipping to boost customer interest.
  3. Test minimum order values: Experiment with different thresholds to maximize sales while maintaining profitability.
  4. Highlight savings: Clearly show customers how much they’ll save to encourage purchases.

2.2.2. Free Shipping

The "Free Shipping Coupon" feature allows you to reward loyal customers by offering free or discounted shipping in exchange for their loyalty points. This encourages repeat purchases while minimizing shipping costs for your customers.

How to Set It Up:

1. Points Cost: Determine how many points a customer must redeem to claim a free shipping coupon.

2. Maximum Shipping Amount: Specify the maximum shipping value that the coupon will cover. For instance, if the maximum amount is set to $10, the coupon will cover shipping costs up to that limit.

Example:

  1. You set the Points Cost to 100 points and the Maximum Shipping Amount to $10.
  2. If a customer redeems 100 points, they receive a free shipping coupon worth up to $10.
  3. If the shipping cost is $8, the entire cost is covered. However, if the shipping cost is $12, the coupon will cover $10, and the customer will pay the remaining $2.

Additional Settings:

3. Choose Country: You can specify where the free shipping coupon applies. Select one or more countries, or choose all countries. You can also deselect countries after selection if needed. This ensures you can tailor your rewards to specific regions or a global audience.

Quick Tips for Free Shipping Rewards:

  1. Be strategic with limits: Set a maximum shipping amount to manage costs while still offering value.
  2. Promote it clearly: Highlight the free shipping reward in your marketing to attract and retain customers.
  3. Focus on key regions: Offer free shipping to specific locations where it's cost-effective and impactful.

2.2.3. Free Product

The "Free Product" settings allow you to reward loyal customers by offering free products, such as a "Buy One Get One" offer.

How to Set It Up:

1. Discount Name: Create a unique name for your free product discount to make it easily identifiable.

2. Choose the Discount Type: You can select one of the following discount types:

  • Fixed Amount Discount: Offer a specific monetary discount, such as $10 off a customer’s order. This discount applies as a fixed value, regardless of the order total.
  • Percentage Discount: Offer a discount as a percentage of the total order value. For example, a 10% discount on a $50 order will give the customer $5 off.

3. Choose Applicable Categories: Specify where the discount can be applied:

  • Specific Collection: Apply the discount to an entire product collection. For example, offer $10 off all products within a specific collection. If a fixed amount is selected, this discount applies across all eligible products in the chosen collection.
  • Specific Product: Apply the discount to one or more selected products. For example, set a 10% discount on specific products only.

Quick Tips for Free Product Rewards:

  1. Choose appealing products: Select popular or complementary items to encourage customers to redeem points.
  2. Promote BOGO deals: Offer “Buy One, Get One Free” on high-demand items to boost sales and loyalty.
  3. Target specific categories: Limit free product rewards to seasonal or surplus inventory to manage costs effectively.
