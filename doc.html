1. Reward race overview

Thank you for choosing the Reward Race loyalty program! With our program, you can offer your customers exciting ways to earn points and keep them engaged with your store.

The Reward Race program is a fun and engaging loyalty system designed to help merchants reward customers for their actions. It encourages customers to interact with your business, earn points, and compete for exciting rewards.

1.1. What is the reward race program ?

The Reward Race program is a fun and engaging loyalty system designed to help merchants reward customers for their actions. It encourages customers to interact with your business, earn points, and compete for exciting rewards.

1.2. What makes us unique

Our system offers four key features— Reward Program, VIP Program, Referral Program, and Survey Program— designed to help merchants easily reward their customers and boost engagement. Here's how each feature works and how you can use them to award points:

  • 1. Reward Program: Encourage everyday customer actions like purchases or social media interactions.
  • Example: A customer spends $50 at your store and earns 500 points, which they can later use to get a 10% discount.
  • 1. VIP Program: Reward your most loyal customers with exclusive benefits. Set different VIP tiers (e.g., Silver, Gold, Platinum) based on customer spending or points earned. Offer extra rewards, such as bonus points, early access to sales, or special gifts for higher tiers.
  • Example: A customer who earns 1,000 points in a month qualifies for the Gold tier and receives double points on all purchases for the next month.
  • 1. Referral Program: Grow your customer base through word-of-mouth marketing. Provide customers with a unique referral code or link. When someone uses their code to make a purchase, both the referrer and the new customer earn points.
  • Example: A customer refers a friend who spends $30. The customer earns 300 points, and the friend gets a welcome bonus of 100 points.
  • 1. Survey Program: Collect valuable feedback while rewarding customers for their input. Create surveys about your products or services. Offer points to customers who complete the surveys, encouraging participation.
  • Example: A customer fills out a feedback form about their shopping experience and earns 50 points.

Tips for Setting Up Sign-Up Points:

  1. Offer a generous but sustainable number of points to encourage sign-ups. For example, 100-200 points can be an attractive starting reward.
  2. Clearly display the reward points on your loyalty page to motivate new customers to join.
  3. Ensure the reward value aligns with your business model. Calculate the points-to-currency ratio to avoid over-rewarding.
  4. Promote your sign-up reward on social media and email campaigns to maximize engagement.
  5. Regularly review and adjust the reward points based on customer feedback and business performance.

2.1.2. Purchase Reward

1. After click this edit button you can find out purchase reward details.

2. You can change the reward name with this option, similar to the Sign-Up Reward feature.

3. The "Purchase Rewards" feature allows customers to earn points every time they make a purchase. You can set specific point values for different purchase amounts, encouraging repeat purchases and increasing customer loyalty.

Reward Options:

  • 1. Every $1 Spent = Points:

    Reward customers based on the amount they spend:

    • Example 1: If you set Every $1 Spent = 1 Point, a $10 purchase earns 10 points.
    • Example 2: If you set Every $1 Spent = 5 Points, a $10 purchase earns 50 points.

    This system directly links rewards to the amount spent, incentivizing higher spending.

  • 2. Fixed Amount of Points:

    Reward customers with a fixed number of points for each transaction, regardless of the purchase amount:

    • Example: If you set a fixed rule of 100 points per transaction, a $10 purchase earns 100 points, and a $50 purchase also earns 100 points.

    This system provides consistent rewards, encouraging frequent transactions rather than higher spending.

Tips for Setting Up Purchase Rewards:

  1. Use the "Every $1 Spent" option to encourage higher spending from customers while maintaining flexibility to adjust the points-to-currency ratio.
  2. Consider the "Fixed Amount of Points" option for low-ticket items or to reward frequent shoppers equally, regardless of purchase value.
  3. Test different reward structures to find what resonates most with your audience—adjust based on feedback and sales performance.
  4. Clearly communicate the rewards program on your loyalty page and at checkout to ensure customers understand the benefits.
  5. Monitor redemption rates to ensure your rewards program remains profitable while engaging your customers effectively.
  6. Use the Survey Program feature to ask customers what they like and need. Their feedback can help you improve your purchase rewards to make them more attractive and useful.

2.2. Free shipping

This "Free Shipping Coupon" option allows you to reward customers by offering free or discounted shipping in exchange for loyalty points.

1. Points Cost: This is the number of points a customer must redeem to receive a free shipping coupon.

2. Set a Maximum Shipping Amount: You can set a limit on the shipping value that the coupon will cover. For example, if the maximum shipping amount is set to 1 BDT, the free shipping coupon will cover shipping costs up to 1 BDT.

Example:

  • Let’s say you set the Points Cost to 100 points, and the Maximum Shipping Amount to 50 BDT.
  • If a customer redeems 100 points, they can get a free shipping coupon that covers shipping fees up to 50 BDT.
  • If their shipping cost is 40 BDT, the entire cost will be covered. However, if the shipping cost is 60 BDT, the coupon will only cover 50 BDT, and the customer will need to pay the remaining 10 BDT.

3. Reward Expiration for Free Shipping Coupon In this section, you can choose whether the free shipping coupon reward should have an expiration date or remain valid permanently. You have two main options:

  1. No Expiration: Select this option if you want the free shipping coupon to remain valid permanently. Customers will be able to redeem the coupon at any time without a set expiration date.
  2. Set Expiration Period: If you prefer to limit how long the coupon is valid, select this option and choose a specific expiration period (e.g., 10 days, 30 days, etc.). This ensures that the coupon can only be used within the specified timeframe, encouraging customers to use the reward promptly.
