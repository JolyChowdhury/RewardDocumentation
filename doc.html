1.3 Rewards on purchase

1. "Rewards on Purchase" feature allows customers to earn points every time they make a purchase. You can set specific point values for different purchase amounts, encouraging repeat buying and increasing customer loyalty.

In the "Rewards on Purchase" feature of your loyalty app, you have two options for rewarding customers:

  • Every $1 Spent = 1 Points: This option allows you to reward customers based on how much they spend.
    • Example 1: A customer makes a purchase of $10. He will earn = 10 dollars × 1 point per dollar = 10 points..
    • Example 2: Another customer makes a purchase of $100. He will earn 100 dollars × 1 point per dollar = 100 points.

    In this system, the points are directly equal to the amount of money spent. For every dollar spent, the customer earns one point. The more a customer spends, the more points they earn.

  • Fixed Amount of Points: With this option, you can reward customers with a set number of points regardless of the purchase amount. For instance, no matter how much a customer spends, they will receive a fixed amount of points for each transaction.

    Let’s say you set a fixed rule where customers will earn 100 points per transaction, no matter how much they spend.

    • Suppose, X makes a purchase of $10 for a product.

    Since the fixed rule says X will always get 100 points for any transaction:

    • X spends $10 → he earns 100 points.
    • If X spends $50, he still earns 100 points.

    In this system, the number of points doesn’t change based on how much X spends; it's a fixed reward for every purchase, regardless of the total amount.

2. Ways to redeem points

The Redeem Points feature allows your customers to exchange their earned points for rewards. This not only encourages repeat purchases but also keeps customers engaged with your loyalty program. To manage the rules for how customers can redeem points,

Go to the Reward Program. There, you’ll find all the ways to redeem points tab actions we offer.

2.1. Purchase discount

This feature allows customers to redeem their points for a discount on their purchases. The discount is based on a points-to-dollar conversion that you set, along with a few additional rules like minimum order value and maximum discount percentage.

How Purchase Discount Works:

1. Points Value:

  • You can set reward name with "set name".
  • You can set how many points are needed to give a discount.
  • Example: For every 100 points, the customer will receive $1 off their purchase.

2. Minimum Order Value:

  • Customers need to meet a minimum order value before they can redeem their points for a discount.
  • Example: In this case, the customer needs to spend at least $50 to be eligible for a discount.

3. Summary

  • After completing all of the setup, you can view the details in the summary

2.2. Free shipping

This "Free Shipping Coupon" option allows you to reward customers by offering free or discounted shipping in exchange for loyalty points.

1. Points Cost: This is the number of points a customer must redeem to receive a free shipping coupon.

2. Set a Maximum Shipping Amount: You can set a limit on the shipping value that the coupon will cover. For example, if the maximum shipping amount is set to 1 BDT, the free shipping coupon will cover shipping costs up to 1 BDT.

Example:

  • Let’s say you set the Points Cost to 100 points, and the Maximum Shipping Amount to 50 BDT.
  • If a customer redeems 100 points, they can get a free shipping coupon that covers shipping fees up to 50 BDT.
  • If their shipping cost is 40 BDT, the entire cost will be covered. However, if the shipping cost is 60 BDT, the coupon will only cover 50 BDT, and the customer will need to pay the remaining 10 BDT.

3. Reward Expiration for Free Shipping Coupon In this section, you can choose whether the free shipping coupon reward should have an expiration date or remain valid permanently. You have two main options:

  1. No Expiration: Select this option if you want the free shipping coupon to remain valid permanently. Customers will be able to redeem the coupon at any time without a set expiration date.
  2. Set Expiration Period: If you prefer to limit how long the coupon is valid, select this option and choose a specific expiration period (e.g., 10 days, 30 days, etc.). This ensures that the coupon can only be used within the specified timeframe, encouraging customers to use the reward promptly.
